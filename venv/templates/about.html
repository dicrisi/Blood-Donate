Blood Donation Rules and Regulations

The age of the donor must be above 18 years and below 65 years of age. He/she must have a haemoglobin count that is not less than 12.5 g/dl. The Weigh should not less than 45 kgs. One should have a normal body temperature at the time of donation

Eligibility Criteria

  • You must be in good general health and feeling well.
  • You must be at least 17 years old (16 years old with parental consent in some states).
  • You must weigh at least 110 lbs (50 kg).
  • You must have not donated blood in the last 56 days.

Health and Lifestyle Restrictions

  • You should not be under the influence of alcohol or drugs at the time of donation.
  • You should not have had a recent tattoo or piercing (within the last 12 months).
  • You should not have any cold or flu symptoms on the day of donation.
  • If you have traveled to a malaria-endemic area, you may need to wait before donating.

Post-Donation Care

  • Drink extra fluids for the next 24-48 hours.
  • Avoid heavy lifting or strenuous exercise for the rest of the day.
  • If you feel lightheaded or dizzy, lie down until you feel better.
  • Keep the bandage on your arm and dry for the next five hours.

Medical Conditions

  • If you have certain chronic illnesses (like heart disease, diabetes, or epilepsy), consult with your healthcare provider before donating.
  • If you are currently taking antibiotics or have an active infection, you should not donate blood.
  • If you have a history of certain medical conditions, such as cancer, hepatitis, or HIV/AIDS, you may be ineligible to donate.

Preparation Before Donation

  • Get a good night's sleep before the day of your donation.
  • Eat a healthy meal before donating, avoiding fatty foods.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Bring a list of medications you are currently taking.
  • Carry a valid ID or donor card.
